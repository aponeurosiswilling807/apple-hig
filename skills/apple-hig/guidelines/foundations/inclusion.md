# Inclusion

> ⚠️ Universal. Re-verify on Apple.

Design for a broad audience and avoid assumptions about people's abilities, language, culture,
age, gender, or circumstances.

## Guidelines

- **Use inclusive, people-first language.** Avoid idioms, slang, and culturally specific
  references that don't translate. Avoid terms with discriminatory history.
- **Represent diversity** in imagery, names, and examples (skin tones, genders, family
  structures, abilities). Use diverse emoji/person symbols where relevant.
- **Don't assume defaults** — avoid gendered assumptions; offer inclusive options (e.g. titles,
  pronouns) and let people self-describe.
- **Localize properly** (not just translate): dates, numbers, currency, names, address formats,
  and **layout direction** (see [[right-to-left]]).
- **Be mindful of color meaning** across cultures; never rely on color alone (see [[accessibility]]).
- **Make accessibility a form of inclusion** — pair with [[accessibility]].
- **Avoid stereotypes and bias**, including in AI-generated content, examples, and defaults.

## Apply it

- Test with people from different backgrounds and abilities.
- Provide neutral, respectful **error and empty states**.
- Let people control how they're addressed and what's shared (see [[privacy]]).
